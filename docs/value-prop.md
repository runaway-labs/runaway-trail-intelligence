# Value Proposition

## Statement

AI Trail Intelligence delivers personalized in-race support to ultra and trail runners, adapting to their pace, terrain, biometrics, and race history to surface the right coaching cue at the right mile. Starting with audio coaching delivered through the athlete's existing devices, ATI gives every runner on the course a coach in their ear who knows the terrain as well as the race director knows it. Race organizations partner with ATI to elevate the athlete experience on remote, hard-to-monitor courses; athletes opt in to deeper personalization on top of the baseline experience their race provides. Built by a marathoner who has architected enterprise AI systems, ATI turns the loneliest miles of a race into the ones athletes remember most.

## Key decisions

- **Primary problem**: Athlete experience and personalized in-race support
- **Beachhead market**: Ultra and trail races (remote, hard to monitor, highly engaged athletes)
- **First delivery channel**: Audio coaching via athlete's existing devices (AirPods, Garmin)
- **Future channels**: Crew-facing dashboard, watch/phone visual support
- **Revenue model**: Hybrid. Race orgs pay per event for baseline experience, athletes opt in to deeper personalization

## Why these decisions

**Why athlete experience over safety**: Race directors care about safety, but athletes care about experience, and athletes are the ones who pay entry fees, share on social, and come back next year. Race orgs win when athletes have a great race. Athlete experience is also more visible and demonstrable, easier to build a case for in a single POC.

**Why ultras/trail as beachhead**: Remote, the field is small enough to personalize, athletes are highly engaged and data-loving, and the existing tech is thin. The "loneliest miles" framing is real and felt — and largely unaddressed.

**Why audio first**: Most differentiated, ultras already involve hours of solo running where athletes are receptive to audio, and it gives the strongest demo. A crew dashboard is easier to build but less defensible. A watch app means fighting Garmin and Apple's app review cycles.

**Why hybrid revenue model**: Race orgs get the badge of providing premium athlete support, athletes get value they care about, and ATI gets two reinforcing revenue lines.
